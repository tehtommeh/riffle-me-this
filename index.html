üîÑ Alternating Top/Bottom Shuffle

Take cards from the original deck one by one (or in groups) and alternately place them on the top and bottom of a new pile.

Example: Cards 1,2,3,4 ‚Üí 1,3 on top, 2,4 on bottom ‚Üí Final order: 3,1,2,4

üîÉ Alternating Bottom/Top Shuffle

Take cards from the original deck one by one (or in groups) and alternately place them on the bottom and top of a new pile.

Example: Cards 1,2,3,4 ‚Üí 1,3 on bottom, 2,4 on top ‚Üí Final order: 4,2,1,3

üÉè Riffle Shuffle

Split the deck in half, then interleave cards from both halves. This mimics the common card shuffling technique where you riffle the two halves together.

Example: Split [1,2,3,4,5,6] ‚Üí [1,2,3] and [4,5,6] ‚Üí Interleave ‚Üí [4,1,5,2,6,3]

üêµ Monkey Shuffle

Randomly pick a card from anywhere in the deck and move it to the top. Named for the chaotic, seemingly random nature of the shuffle.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] ‚Üí randomly pick card 4 ‚Üí [4,1,2,3,5,6]

üßµ Weave Shuffle

Take every nth card from the deck (based on skip distance) and move them to the end, preserving their relative order.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] with skip=3 ‚Üí take every 3rd: [3,6] ‚Üí result: [1,2,4,5,3,6]

‚ú® Perfect Faro Shuffle

Mathematically perfect riffle shuffle. Split exactly in half and interleave with perfect precision. Out-shuffle keeps top card on top, in-shuffle moves it to second position.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] ‚Üí split [1,2,3][4,5,6] ‚Üí out-shuffle: [1,4,2,5,3,6]

üè∞ Pile Shuffle

Deal cards one by one into multiple piles, then collect the piles in order. Creates interesting periodic patterns based on pile count.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] into 3 piles ‚Üí [1,4][2,5][3,6] ‚Üí collect: [1,4,2,5,3,6]

üåÄ Spiral Shuffle

Place cards at positions determined by mathematical sequences like Fibonacci or prime numbers. Creates complex, mathematically interesting patterns.

Example: Using Fibonacci positions (1,1,2,3,5...) to determine where each card goes in the new arrangement.

üîÅ Reverse Sections

Split the deck into equal sections and reverse the order of cards within each section, keeping sections in the same position.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] into 3 sections ‚Üí [2,1][4,3][6,5] ‚Üí result: [2,1,4,3,6,5]

‚úÇÔ∏è Cut Shuffle

Cut the deck at a specific position and put the bottom portion on top. This is the simplest shuffle - just moving one section above another.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] cut at 50% ‚Üí [1,2,3] and [4,5,6] ‚Üí Put bottom on top ‚Üí [4,5,6,1,2,3]

üé£ Fisherman Shuffle

Alternately take cards from the very top and very bottom of the deck, building a new pile. Named for resembling how a fisherman might sort through a catch.

Example: [1,2,3,4,5,6] ‚Üí Take top(1), bottom(6), top(2), bottom(5) ‚Üí New order: [1,6,2,5,3,4]
